Most existing recruitment web portal focuses on serving the needs of huge or small enterprises for gulf countries that have a separate HR team and are regularly hiring private drivers from india. They try to hire candidates from various online platforms like naukri.com or indeed but managing a hiring process can cause a real headache.
To somehow track applicants, businesses usually use free of charge tools like Excel, or are trying to find their own way to cope with the issue.

  • Experiencing the pain of structuring and managing candidates firsthand, our ux designing team came up with the idea to help gulf recruiters by creating website that would make the hiring process simple, easy, and clear.
  • This website allows recruiters to fully solve the problem of hiring starting from posting the candidate details on website to sending the right candidate successfully to the recruiters.

With the help of this website, recruiters can easily manage :

The candidate list
The user dashboard
Track candidate

Making the website clean, minimalist, but valuable and easy from the user's standpoint

As all similar software on the market looks a bit outdated and complex, the main idea our client came to us with was to make this website both visually pleasing and easy.

To be able to meet the above requirements, we carried out research with the goal to understand how to make website convenient and helpful, but not overloaded with too much functionality or unnecessary design elements.

Contextual inquiry

An essential and very productive part of the research phase was conducting contextual inquiry. We were trying to design something which is more like an e-commerce web app.

The study aims to uncover user behaviors, pain points, and preferences to identify opportunities for enhancing the user experience and optimizing the design of the web app.

Participant Information:
  • Participant's Name: Abdul Kalam Seikh
  • Role: HR Manager
  • Experience Level: Intermediate
Session Details:
  • Date: 20th July 2022
  • Time: 11:00AM
  • Location: Mumbai
  • Duration: 6hrs.
Goals:
  1. Understand the participant's role and responsibilities related to candidate hiring.
  2. Explore how the participant currently performs candidate hiring tasks.
  3. Identify pain points and usability issues in using the Candidate Hiring Web App.
  4. Collect feedback on features and functionalities that are desirable or missing.
  5. Observe the participant's navigation and interaction with the app to understand their workflow.
Tasks:
  1. Logging In: Ask the participant to log into the Candidate Hiring Web App using their provided credentials.
  2. Searching New Candidate: Request the participant to search for required candidate or candidate list.
  3. Reviewing User Dashboard: Ask the participant to log into the Candidate Hiring Web App using their provided credentials.
  4. Exploring Reporting and Analytics: Instruct the participant to explore the reporting and analytics section of the app, where they can see metrics related to their hiring activities.
Observation:
  • Participant's interaction with the app (e.g., clicks, scrolls, inputs).
  • Participant's facial expressions, comments, frustrations, and moments of ease.
  • Any areas where the participant hesitates, expresses confusion, or encounters difficulties.
  • Suggestions or feature requests made by the participant.
Conclusion:

At the end of the session, thank the participant for their time and insights. Compile the observations and feedback gathered during the session to identify usability issues and areas for improvement in the Candidate Hiring Web App. We use this information to refine the app's design and functionality, making it more user-friendly and effective for candidate hiring processes.

Building wireframes

The next step was to visualize the insights we got at the research stage with the help of wireframing.

Wireframes help to define the basic structure and cont+ent layout of each screen before creating a visual design. This way we could take into account both customer needs and user journey at the initial stage of product development.

What we got at the outcome

Our intention was to simplify each stage in the user flow to make interaction with the app just a piece of cake. For that reason, we not only created the minimalistic user interface but also designed many intuitive features that allow users to accomplish their goals fast.

To be able to meet the above requirements, we carried out research with the goal to understand how to make website convenient and helpful, but not overloaded with too much functionality or unnecessary design elements.

Homepage:

The homepage is the first screen the user sees when they open the application. Its purpose is to give the viewer a quick overview of the most important information.

User Dashboard:

In user dashboard, user can easily track the progress and results of their bookings. We decided to divide the user dashboard into six tabs: Profile setting, My Orders, Wishlist, Password & Security, Notification and Logout.

Candidate List:

A user-friendly candidate list allows users to view all candidates and user can select and book suitable candidate according to their requirements.

Progress and results:

Users can easily track the progress and results of the candidates through all stages (applied, screened, medical clearance, Emigration, ticket booking details, and hired).

Mailbox:

To quickly get in touch with appliers, we integrated the mailbox to exchange emails with appliers, without leaving the applicant tracking system.

Software that simplifies the way you recruit

Words “clean” and “simple” went through our whole product design process. When designing each screen, Our team strived to create an intuitive and minimalistic UI, get rid of all unnecessary elements, and design features that would shorten users’ time-to-hire and make the hiring process easy and pleasant.
